Our Mission

Honest Reviews in an Industry Full of Hype

The supplement industry generates over $150 billion annually — and most of that is fueled by marketing, not science. Proprietary blends hide underdosed ingredients. Flashy ads make ordinary products sound revolutionary. Review sites rank products based on affiliate payout, not efficacy.

FitLabReviews exists to cut through that noise. The goal is simple: help you make better purchasing decisions with your health and money.

When a product is recommended here, it's because it genuinely delivers on its claims — not because the brand pays a high commission rate. When a product doesn't live up to the hype, we say that clearly, even if it costs us revenue.

How We Review

Our Methodology

Every supplement goes through a structured 5-step process before a FitLab Score is assigned. No exceptions.

1
Ingredient Analysis

Every ingredient is checked against current peer-reviewed literature for evidence of efficacy, bioavailability, and safety at the dose used. Ingredient form matters — magnesium oxide vs glycinate, cyanocobalamin vs methylcobalamin — and is evaluated accordingly.

2
Dosing Accuracy Check

Serving sizes and dosages are compared against the clinically effective doses established in research. Underdosed "fairy dust" ingredients are flagged and penalised. The label dose and the clinical dose are two different numbers — and we check both.

3
Label Transparency Audit

Proprietary blends lose points. Products with full label disclosure — every ingredient, every dose — are scored higher. Third-party testing certifications (NSF, Informed Sport, Trustified, FSSAI) are verified and noted.

4
Real-World Testing

Products are tested personally where possible — including MuscleBlaze, ON Gold Standard, and other India-available products used regularly. Taste, mixability, and actual performance over 4–8 weeks are factored into the score.

5
Value Assessment

Cost per effective dose is calculated and compared to alternatives in the same category. A great formula that costs 3× its competitors has points deducted. INR pricing is assessed for all India-market products.

Scoring System

How FitLab Scores Are Calculated

Each product is scored out of 10 using a weighted system. Weights reflect clinical importance, not marketing value.

30%
